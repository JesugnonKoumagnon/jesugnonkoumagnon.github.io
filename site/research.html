2024 Lake Chad Basin
Cameroon · Chad · Niger · Nigeria Working Paper

Boko Haram Violence and Climate Change in the Lake Chad Region

A district- and grid-cell-level panel study (2009–2022) testing whether climate anomalies — temperature, rainfall, vegetation, and drought — have a measurable relationship with the intensity and spread of Boko Haram violence.

Key finding Warmer-than-average temperatures and drought conditions (low SPEI) are associated with significantly higher conflict intensity at the district level, while higher rainfall is associated with less conflict. Violence also shows strong spatial spillover — conflict in neighboring districts is one of the strongest predictors of local conflict.

Method

  • 92 districts + grid cells (~10km²) across the Lake Chad basin, 2009–2022
  • Conflict data from ACLED; climate data from MODIS (NDVI, temperature) and CHIRPS (rainfall)
  • Fixed-effects panel regression with annual climate anomalies (z-scores) and a spatially lagged conflict term
  • Controls: population (WorldPop), travel time to cities (Malaria Atlas Project), night-time lights, cropland share
  • Addressed reverse causality (lagged variables), omitted variable bias (control set), and spatial autocorrelation (spatial FE + lag)

2024 Nigeria
37 States, 6 Geopolitical Zones Policy Brief

Multidimensional Poverty and Social Protection Assistance in Nigeria

A state-level diagnostic asking whether Nigeria's three major social assistance programs (P-CGS, VGF, CCT) reach the households who need them most.

Key finding They largely don't. States with the highest poverty (MPI above 80% — Sokoto, Bayelsa, Gombe, Jigawa, Plateau) receive the lowest program coverage, while some moderate-poverty states show National Social Register enrolment exceeding 100% of their poor population — a sign of likely misclassification. 47.4% of Nigeria's poor are not registered at all.

Method

  • Merged NBS state-level Multidimensional Poverty Index data with National Social Register and program beneficiary figures
  • Built a choropleth map of poverty incidence across all 37 states
  • Computed coverage gaps (poor population vs. registered population) by state and geopolitical zone
  • Cross-tabulated program reach (P-CGS, VGF, CCT) against poverty severity to surface targeting mismatches

2024–2025 Côte d'Ivoire
UNICEF / Ministry of Economy, Plan & Development Government Report

N-MODA: National Multiple Overlapping Deprivation Analysis on Child Poverty

As a Social Policy Intern at UNICEF Côte d'Ivoire, I led the data analysis underlying the 3rd edition of the country's official child poverty report — based on EDS 2021 survey data, covering 5,000+ households.

Key finding Multidimensional child poverty in Côte d'Ivoire shows strong regional concentration in the North, North-East, and West — the same regions where climate vulnerability indices are highest — pointing to compounding deprivation that monetary poverty measures alone miss.

Contribution

  • Analyzed multidimensional deprivation data across health, water, sanitation, education, and housing for 5,000+ households
  • Mapped MPI distribution and changes in deprivation across districts
  • Produced a policy brief with 10+ recommendations for government social programs
  • Built an interactive R Shiny dashboard tracking 20+ social indicators for UNICEF teams

Data sources

Key datasets, explained.

ACLED

Armed Conflict Location & Event Data — globally tracked political violence and protest events, geo-coded at the incident level.

MODIS

NASA's Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer — satellite-derived vegetation (NDVI) and temperature data at ~5km resolution.

CHIRPS

Climate Hazards Group InfraRed Precipitation with Station data — high-resolution monthly rainfall estimates used to detect drought.

SPEI

Standardized Precipitation-Evapotranspiration Index — a drought severity measure combining rainfall and temperature across time scales.

MPI

Multidimensional Poverty Index — measures deprivation across health, education, and living standards, complementing income-based poverty.

WorldPop

Gridded population estimates at ~100m resolution, used here to control for population density in conflict and poverty models.
